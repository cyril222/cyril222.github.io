Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish known for its flavorful tomato-based rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, blended into a puree
  • 1 bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, green beans)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until translucent.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and bell pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in tomato puree, thyme, curry powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Cook for about 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil separates.
  4. Add rice to the pot and stir to coat with the sauce. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.
  6. Stir in mixed vegetables. Cover and let sit for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork before serving.

Kelewele (Spicy Fried Plantains)

Kelewele is a popular Ghanaian street food made from spicy fried plantains.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe plantains, peeled and cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine ginger, cayenne pepper, black pepper, and salt.
  2. Toss plantain chunks in the spice mixture until evenly coated.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or skillet over medium heat.
  4. Fry the seasoned plantains in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per batch.
  5. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve hot as a snack or side dish.

Groundnut Soup (Ghanaian Peanut Soup)

Groundnut Soup is a hearty Ghanaian dish made with peanut butter and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup smooth peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup chopped spinach or kale
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  2. Add tomatoes and bell pepper. Cook for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in ground coriander, cumin, paprika, thyme, and chili powder. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add peanut butter to the pot, stirring until melted and combined with the vegetables.
  5. Gradually pour in the broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  6. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the soup thickens slightly.
  7. Stir in chopped spinach or kale. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes until greens are wilted and flavors are well combined.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with rice or fufu (Ghanaian dough).
